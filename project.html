Prayagraj

The City of Sangam

About Prayagraj

Prayagraj is a historic and spiritual city in Uttar Pradesh. It is famous for Triveni Sangam, where the Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati rivers meet. The city is known for its culture, history, and religious importance.

Famous Places

  • Triveni Sangam – Holy meeting point of three rivers
  • Allahabad Fort – Built by Akbar near Sangam
  • Khusro Bagh – Beautiful garden with historical tombs
  • Anand Bhavan – Nehru family museum
  • Hanuman Mandir – Famous temple near Sangam

Famous Food

  • Kachori Sabzi
  • Samosa & Chaat
  • Jalebi
  • Kulfi & Falooda
  • Lassi
  • Chole Bhature

Festivals

  • Kumbh Mela – Largest religious gathering
  • Diwali – Festival of lights

Why Visit?

Prayagraj offers a mix of spirituality, history, and delicious food. It is a must-visit place for tourists and devotees.

Varanasi

The Spiritual Capital of India

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is one of the oldest living cities in the world. It is located on the banks of the Ganga River and is famous for its spiritual atmosphere, temples, and ghats. It is an important religious center for Hindus.

Famous Places

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple – One of the most famous Shiva temples
  • Dashashwamedh Ghat – Known for Ganga Aarti
  • Assi Ghat – Popular ghat for tourists and locals
  • Sarnath – Place where Buddha gave his first sermon
  • Banaras Hindu University – One of Asia’s largest universities

Famous Food

  • Kachori Sabzi
  • Banarasi Paan
  • Chaat
  • Malaiyyo (Winter Special)
  • Lassi
  • Jalebi

Festivals

  • Dev Deepawali – Grand celebration on ghats
  • Maha Shivratri – Dedicated to Lord Shiva

Why Visit?

Varanasi offers a unique blend of spirituality, culture, and tradition. The mesmerizing Ganga Aarti and ancient temples make it a must-visit destination.

Mathura

The Birthplace of Lord Krishna

Mathura is a sacred city in Uttar Pradesh and is known as the birthplace of Lord Krishna. It is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus and is rich in culture, history, and spirituality.

Famous Places

  • Krishna Janmabhoomi – Birthplace of Lord Krishna
  • Dwarkadhish Temple – Famous Krishna temple
  • Vishram Ghat – Sacred bathing ghat
  • Govardhan Hill – Religious site near Mathura
  • Gita Mandir – Temple with Bhagavad Gita inscriptions

Famous Food

  • Peda (Mathura Peda)
  • Kachori Sabzi
  • Jalebi
  • Lassi
  • Chaat
  • Rabri

Festivals

  • Janmashtami – Birth of Lord Krishna
  • Holi – Famous colorful celebration

Why Visit?

Mathura offers a divine experience with temples, ghats, and festivals. It is a must-visit for devotees and those interested in Indian culture.
